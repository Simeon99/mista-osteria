# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local diners deciding where to eat tonight: people nearby, weighing options right now, on a phone more often than a desktop. They're not doing deep research; they're scanning for a fast, confident "yes, this is the place" signal (what makes it worth choosing over the pizzeria down the street) and then acting on it immediately (calling, checking hours, finding the address). Secondary audience: people planning a special dinner further ahead, who want the same confidence but read a little more before committing.

## Product Purpose

A single landing page for Mista Osteria, an Italian pizza restaurant, that makes the case for choosing this specific place tonight and converts that decision into a reservation call. It exists to carry the restaurant's actual craft story (72-hour dough fermentation, Italian-sourced ingredients, wood-fired cooking) in a way that reads as premium and considered rather than templated, and to get the essentials (menu highlights with prices, hours, address, phone) in front of a diner within seconds of landing.

Success looks like: a visitor understands what makes this osteria worth visiting within one scroll of the hero, and can find the phone number / reservation path without hunting.

## Brand Personality

Minimalist, editorial, premium-warm. Restrained terracotta-and-cream palette, magazine-style serif/sans typography pairing, generous whitespace. Confidence comes from craft specifics (the 72h fermentation, the named Italian ingredients, the wood oven) stated plainly, not from loud visual noise. Warmth is carried by photography, copy, and the accent color, not by decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-slop SaaS look**: no purple/blue gradients, no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, no identical three-card grids, no gradient text, no glassmorphism used decoratively, no "hero-metric" stat blocks. This is the primary thing to avoid.
- Cheap/budget pizzeria clichés (checkered tablecloth motifs, clip-art chef hats, playful/cartoonish type) — this is a premium osteria, not a takeout counter.
- Corporate/chain-restaurant polish (stock-photo-perfect food, franchise sterility) — should read as one specific, real, family-run place, using the restaurant's own photography.

## Design Principles

1. **Craft specifics over decoration.** The 72-hour fermentation, the named Italian ingredients, the wood oven — these concrete facts are the design's real leverage. Let them carry the premium feeling instead of ornamental effects.
2. **One scroll to conviction.** A diner deciding "tonight" should get the case for this place (hero + featured dishes) before being asked to work for it.
3. **Essentials never buried.** Hours, address, phone, and a reservation path stay easy to find, not sacrificed for atmosphere.
4. **Restraint reads as premium.** One accent color, one type pairing, generous whitespace. Every added visual device has to earn its place against that baseline.
5. **Photography is the brand voice.** Real restaurant photography (interior, dough, oven, dishes) does the emotional work; copy stays concrete and unembellished rather than compensating with adjectives.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Light theme only, WCAG AA contrast minimum against the `#FCF7F3` background (verified per color pairing: body text/ink ~14.6:1, accent-strong buttons ~5.6:1, footer text on dark ~16.8:1). All motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion` (scroll reveals and press states degrade to instant/no-motion). Copy is Serbian throughout; all imagery has descriptive Serbian alt text.
